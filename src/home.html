The menu bar on the official Simcast website is of a dark purple shade that can be straining on the eyes. There are also no icons to represent the menu options. In the ADHD counterpart of this website there is a retractable menu bar that contains intuitive icons, making the menu bar easier to navigate. Moreover, instead of splitting the webpage into two sections, one containing the news segments and another the login page, the latter has been added to the menu bar so that ADHD users would not be confused and distracted by the large amount of information at hand.

On the official website, the title can be found in the corner of the webpage in a small unnoticeable font. Instead the ADHD friendly version of this website contains a title with a large, Sans font that can easily be distinguished as the title of the webpage. The text is a shade of dark grey making it easer for the user to read the text.

On the official Simcast website the 'trending news' and 'breaking news' segments are cramped onto one page. On the ADHD accessible version of this website the two segments have been split up and will be presented separately as such a vast amount of information can prove to be distracting for an ADHD user.

There was no toolbar on the official Simcast website. However, the ADHD accessible counterpart contains a note-taking, highlighting and read-aloud functions. All three functions make it easier for an ADHD user to retain large amounts of information.
